the distance between two cities a and b is 465 km . a train starts from a at 8 a . m . and travel towards b at 60 km / hr . another train starts from b at 9 a . m and travels towards a at 75 km / hr . at what time do they meet ?

First, I need to determine how far the first train from city A has traveled by the time the second train from city B starts at 9 a.m.Since the first train starts at 8 a.m. and travels for 1 hour before the second train starts, I can calculate the distance it covers in that hour:Distance covered by the first train in 1 hour = speed time= 60 km/hr 1 hr= 60 kmSo, by 9 a.m., the first train is 60 km away from city A, which means the remaining distance between the two trains is:Remaining distance = total distance - distance covered by the first train= 465 km - 60 km= 405 kmNow, I need to calculate the relative speed at which the two trains are approaching each other. Since they are moving towards each other, I add their speeds:Relative speed = speed of the first train + speed of the second train= 60 km/hr + 75 km/hr= 135 km/hrNow I can calculate the time it takes for the two trains to meet by dividing the remaining distance by their relative speed:Time to meet = remaining distance / relative speed= 405 km / 135 km/hr= 3 hoursSince the second train starts at 9 a.m., they will meet 3 hours after 9 a.m., which is:9 a.m. + 3 hours = 12 p.m. (noon)Therefore, the two trains will meet at 12 p.m. (noon).
